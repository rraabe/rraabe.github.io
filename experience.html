Career Highlights

2003 - 2007:
Undergraduate degree in Biochemistry and Molecular Biology at the University of Richmond.

2008 - 2010:
Developed a cost-efficient, rapid, point-of-care influenza test for use in clinical settings. Redesigned the extraction buffer and immunoassay capture system. Scaled for production and reduced cost per test.

2011 - 2014:
Master's degree in Pharmacology and Toxicology. Published discovery that genes controlling fatty acid production as well as fatty acid content can alter the response to ethanol in C. elegans.

2014 - 2017:
Developed and validated ELISAs to test clinical trial samples for therapeutic levels and the presence of anti-therapeutic antibodies. Worked closely with production analysts to design user-friendly methods which increased efficiency, decreased manual labor, and reduced error rates.

2017 - 2019:
Moved to Burlington, VT and started testing raw and finished water samples for LT2 water testing per the EPA.

June 2019:
Started the Burlington Code Academy boot camp taking the first step in a new journey of learning and problem-solving.
